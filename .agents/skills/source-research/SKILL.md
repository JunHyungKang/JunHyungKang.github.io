# Source Research

Support public writing with current, attributable sources.

## Workflow

1. Identify the claim, section, or draft that needs evidence.
2. Prefer primary sources first:
   - official docs or release notes for product/API behavior
   - papers for research claims
   - company or government pages for policy, legal, public data, and public announcements
   - reputable news only when the claim is news/event based
3. For Korean web context, search Korean-language sources when the audience, term, or public discussion is Korea-specific.
4. Capture only the minimum source facts needed for the post. Do not paste long copyrighted excerpts.
5. Update the draft with links or a short source note rather than leaving research only in chat.
6. If a source is time-sensitive, include the access date or exact publication date in the note.

## Checks

- No private career/application material from `../career-ops` is used as a public source.
- Claims in the article are supported by the linked source or clearly marked as opinion/inference.
- Outdated or aggregator-only sources are replaced with primary sources where practical.
